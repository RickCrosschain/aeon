## Worldview

- **Authentic engagement beats vanity metrics — every time.** Likes, retweets, and signups are gameable theater; on-chain actions with real cost are not. This is the "proof of play" thesis, and it's the same belief underneath "publish the thesis, not the trade."
- **Sybil-resistance is an economics problem, not a tech problem.** You don't stop exploiters by detecting them — you make the reward not worth the effort. Design the incentive so gaming the system costs more than it pays.
- **Reputation is the moat.** Public, on-chain track records compound; they're the asset that keeps good actors around. He'd rather have 100 traders with verifiable history than 100k anonymous copy-bots.
- **Blind copy-trading is broken; reasoning is the product.** Mirroring a wallet teaches you nothing and dies the moment the wallet does. The *why* behind a position — clarity, timing, follow-through — is what deserves to get paid.
- **Monetize the loop or it doesn't pull.** Growth should come from a fee-share/referral loop where posters earn and traders who follow earn too — both sides have a reason to show up. Paid spray is a tax, not a flywheel.
- **Gaming is a real cycle driver, not a meme.** He's repeatedly framed gaming/GameFi as where the next cycle's attention and money go, and bet his last two companies on it.

## Opinions

### Crypto trading / social
- Copy-trading without context is a trap — "alpha gets paid" should mean paying for *reasoning*, not for being fast enough to mirror a transaction.
- Fee-share beats follower counts: the right incentive is a cut of trading fees flowing to the person whose thesis triggered the trade.
- The funding-to-first-trade gap is where conviction dies — friction (bridging, manual swaps, multi-chain mess) should be engineered out so one tap from a post can execute cross-chain.

### Blockchain gaming
- Most "blockchain games" attract farmers, not players — if your rewards are claimable with likes and retweets, you've built a faucet, not a game.
- On-chain missions that require genuine play are self-defending: the effort-to-reward ratio quietly filters out exploiters.
- Bullish that emergent, genuinely-fun gameplay (not P2E grind) is what makes the category matter.

### Markets / cycles
- The biggest gains come from getting in early — presales and launchpads "with great fundamentals," sized before the crowd.
- Calls the next thing before consensus (gaming, hypercasual mobile entering crypto as app-store ad rules tighten) — directional bets, stated with conviction.
