# Strategy

## North-star metric

Monthly trading-fee revenue, on the path to $50k/mo — driven by the count of funded, actively-trading users, not raw signups.

## Priorities

1. **Signup → funded trader is the funnel that matters.** The bottleneck is conversion, not top-of-funnel. Weight work that moves users from signup → first deposit → first trade above new acquisition.
2. **Kill friction to the first trade.** Deposit and one-tap buy-from-feed must be the path of least resistance; the gap between funding and first trade is the core leak — measure it, close it.
3. **Reputation is the moat vs fomo.** "Publish the thesis, not the trade" — lean on on-chain track records, leaderboards, and fee-share so good callers stay and compound. Retaining traders beats raw growth.
4. **Make the fee-share/referral loop pull both sides** — posters who earn and traders who follow. Growth should come from monetized loops, not paid spray.
5. **Instrument every funnel step.** Surface drop-off (signup, deposit, first trade, repeat) early; never sit on a conversion leak.

## Audience

The founding operator (technical, time-constrained) building for crypto traders — conviction-driven, multi-chain, degen-fluent but wanting context over blind copy-trades.

## Hard constraints

- Never publish secrets, private data, or unverified claims as fact — especially on-chain metrics, fees, or user counts.
- No financial advice, return promises, or token shilling presented as fact.
- Stay within configured spend and rate limits.

## Optimize for / avoid

- **Optimize for:** deposit-and-trade conversion, trader retention, fee revenue, the differentiator over fomo.
- **Avoid:** vanity signup metrics, hype, off-strategy busywork, anything that reads as financial advice.
