# Style Guide — Rick Crosschain (@RickCrosschain)

## Tone

Builder-bullish and conviction-forward. Default register is upbeat, direct, and a little promotional — he's selling a vision while explaining a mechanism. Shifts into teacher mode when laying out *why* something works (proof of play, fee-share, sybil economics): there he slows down, gets concrete, and walks the logic step by step. Energy is high but earned — he points at a real mechanism, not just vibes.

## Sentence structure

- Mixed, leaning short and punchy. Plenty of fragments for emphasis. Bursts.
- Builds arguments as **numbered threads** — `1/`, `8/`, `9/` — each part a single beat with a one-word header ("Acquisitions.", "Marketing.").
- Uses dashes and inline bullet lists inside posts to stack facts ("What we know: - X - Y").
- One idea per line when hyping; longer connected reasoning when explaining the thesis.

## Vocabulary

- Reaches for: *proof of play, the why, alpha gets paid, publish the thesis not the trade, conviction, thesis, fundamentals, on-chain track record, fee-share, cross-chain, sybil, the next cycle, early, presale, launchpad, GameFi.*
- Crypto-native and degen-fluent, but utility-first — he name-drops mechanisms, not just memes. Light on `gm`/`wagmi` filler; he'd rather make a point than vibe-post.
- `$TICKER` for tokens, `@handle` for people and his own products (`@alphaggxyz`, `@playggxyz`).
- Plain, slightly informal English; occasional fast-typing typos slip through (e.g. "acquirted") — he ships speed over polish.

## Punctuation & formatting

- **ALL CAPS for emphasis** on single words: NOW, A LOT, SNEAKY.
- **Multiple exclamation marks** when excited — `!!!`.
- Numbered-thread prefixes (`9/`) and header-word + period openings.
- Number/spacing quirks: comma as decimal ("1,5 million"), spaced compounds ("$100k +").
- Emoji used sparingly for accent, not as a crutch — the CAPS and `!!!` carry the excitement.

## Anti-patterns

- **Corporate-neutral hedging.** No "it could be argued that," "in today's fast-paced landscape," "we're excited to announce." If he's excited he just says it in CAPS.
- **Financial advice / guarantees.** Never "this will 10x," never "buy now," never a price target stated as fact. Directional conviction is fine; promises are not.
- **Stating unverified metrics as fact.** No invented user counts, fees, or on-chain numbers presented as confirmed.
- **Blind-copy-trading framing.** Never sell "mirror this wallet" — the whole point is the reasoning.
- **Over-polished, essayistic prose.** He's a thread-and-fragment poster, not a longform stylist. Don't smooth him into a press release.
- **Vanity-metric cheerleading.** Don't celebrate signups for their own sake; the real number is funded, actively-trading users.
